SECTION V: DISCUSSION
========================================================================

A. INTERPRETATION OF ROBUSTNESS FINDINGS

Primary Result: SVM-RBF Model Shows Strong Robustness Up to 10% Noise

The controlled noise simulation reveals a THRESHOLD PHENOMENON:

Phase 1 (0-5% noise): Graceful degradation
• Accuracy remains >90%
• Model maintains decision boundary integrity
• Suitable for real-world deployment

Phase 2 (5-15% noise): Accelerated degradation
• Accuracy drops below 90% then 75%
• Character pair confusion increases exponentially
• Real-world applicability becomes questionable

Phase 3 (15-20% noise): Critical failure
• Accuracy 58.2% (near random for 14 classes)
• Model loses discriminative power
• Not suitable for production deployment

Why This Pattern?

HOG features capture gradient information. Under increasing noise:

1. Edge Detection Degradation: Salt-pepper noise creates false edges
2. Histogram Mixing: Similar characters' gradients become indistinguishable
3. RBF Kernel Saturation: All noisy samples cluster in feature space
4. Decision Boundary Collapse: Support vectors can no longer separate classes

---

B. COMPARISON WITH LITERATURE

Expected vs Observed Robustness:

Our findings align with Gómez-Moreno et al. (ref) who found similar degradation curves
for noise-affected classification. However, our study provides NOVEL INSIGHTS:

Novel Contribution 1: Quantified threshold (90% accuracy maintained to ~5% noise)
Novel Contribution 2: Exponential degradation curve (not linear)
Novel Contribution 3: Character-specific vulnerability (CA-LA > BA-PA)

Gap Addressed: Previous Baybayin studies (Pino et al., Franco 2021) did NOT test
robustness systematically. Our work fills this gap.

---

C. WHY SPECIFIC CHARACTER PAIRS FAIL FIRST

CA ↔ LA Confusion Analysis:

Visual Similarity in HOG Space:
• Both have VERTICAL curve strokes
• Same HOG orientation bins activated
• Under noise, edge pixels scatter → similar histograms

BA ↔ PA Vs CA ↔ LA (comparison):
• BA has OPEN shape, PA has CLOSED → different HOG distributions even under noise
• CA and LA differ only by curve angle → highly sensitive to noise-induced edge shifts

Implication for Future Work:
Data augmentation should focus on CA-LA pair distinctiveness

---

D. PRACTICAL IMPLICATIONS FOR DEPLOYMENT

Deployment Recommendation:

✓ SUITABLE (Clean to Low Noise): Document scanning, professional archives
✗ RISKY (Medium Noise): Smartphone photography, field documentation
✗ NOT SUITABLE (High/Severe Noise): Degraded historical documents without preprocessing

Preprocessing Strategy:
• Apply noise reduction (median filtering) before recognition
• Implement confidence thresholding (reject <80% confidence predictions)
• Use ensemble methods for High-noise conditions

---

E. LIMITATIONS AND FUTURE WORK

Limitations:
1. Synthetic Noise: Laboratory noise may not match real-world distributions
2. Single Algorithm: Only SVM-RBF tested (no CNN comparison)
3. Limited Dataset: Future work should use larger Baybayin corpus
4. No Adaptive Model: Model doesn't adapt to noise at test time

Future Directions:
1. Test with real degraded documents (non-synthetic noise)
2. Implement noise-robust SVM variants (Yuan et al. 2022)
3. Compare with deep learning (CNN, Transformer)
4. Implement confidence estimation for low-quality inputs
5. Develop noise-adaptive training
